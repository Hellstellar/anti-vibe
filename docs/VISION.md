# Vision / Positioning

Working notes on what this app is really *for* — beyond "a speed-reader." Fold
into the README when ready.

## The problem

LLMs and agents generate large volumes of text. Reviewing it has real cost:

- **Reading fatigue** — long documents on screen are tiring; eyes scan back and
  forth, attention drifts.
- **Inertia to review** — a wall of generated text is daunting, so review gets
  skipped or rushed. The friction of *starting* is the barrier.
- **Slow review speed** — reading linearly at normal pace doesn't scale to the
  amount of AI output people now produce.
- **Screen time** — more generated content means more hours staring at it.

## The angle

Reduce the *cost* of reviewing, not just the time:

- **Lower fatigue** — RSVP keeps the eyes still (content moves, gaze doesn't);
  the spotlight + CRT fade limit what's on screen at once, easing the load.
- **Lower inertia** — section-by-section navigation means you commit to one small
  chunk at a time instead of facing the whole document. Glance at a heading,
  decide, move on or speed-read it.
- **Faster review** — opt-in RSVP per section blasts through the parts worth it.
- **Less screen time** — faster, less fatiguing review = fewer hours on screen.

There's a **health/wellbeing angle** here: review tooling that's gentler on the
eyes and attention, not just faster.

## Where it could go

- Comprehension/retention aids (recall checkpoints, summaries per section).
- Eye-strain-aware pacing, break reminders, session stats (time saved, screen
  time reduced).
- Beyond markdown: PDFs, web articles, email, code review.
- Accessibility framing (dyslexia-friendly RSVP, focus support).
